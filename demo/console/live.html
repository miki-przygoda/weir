The scenario

The whole point of weir is to sit between a fast producer and a slower/flakier sink without ever blocking the producer or losing an acked record. This view is the proof you can see: the front of the pipeline (accept → fsync → ack) running at the producer's rate, and the back (drain → sink) keeping the backlog shallow.

The animation below is driven by a sample metrics feed whose counters climb like a real busy daemon's — so the dots flow and the sparklines fill.

What you're looking at

Counter cards (top) — accepted / ack / nack per second (computed from the deltas between polls), queue depth (drain backlog), average fsync ms, sink health + type, dead-letter bytes, and an alarms chip.

The pipeline canvas — dots spawn at the producer at the real accepted/sec and flow left-to-right; the Drain stage shows the queue backlog and the Sink is tinted by sink health. An idle daemon (0/s) shows no dots — honestly still.

Sparklines — a short rolling history of accepted/sec and queue depth, so you can see a burst or a stall at a glance.

Honest scope

The daemon exposes only a pull /metrics endpoint — no per-record event stream — so Live shows polled rates (sampled ~1.5s, deltas → rates), not individual records, and the drain→sink stage reflects queue depth + sink health rather than a per-second drain rate. A counter reset (daemon restart) re-baselines instead of showing a negative rate; with no daemon, it shows a clean "waiting…" state and resumes automatically.

Why it matters

It's a live, pipeline-shaped view of a real daemon — "watch your durable log breathe." weir also speaks Prometheus, so Grafana owns the time-series dashboards; Live is the complementary narrative view that makes the flow legible at a glance.

The real tool

This page is a sample-data mock (no backend). Live polls a running daemon's metrics:

cargo run --manifest-path tools/weir-console/Cargo.toml -- \
  --wab-dir <dir> --metrics-addr 127.0.0.1:9185
# open the Live tab; it polls /metrics and animates the pipeline at the real rate

weir-console — one local tool, three views (Explorer · Ops · Live). Live reads the same metrics summary the CLI's weir-ctl metrics prints. Full reference at the docs site.
